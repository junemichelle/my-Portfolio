Pomodoro

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It involves breaking work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. Here's how it typically works: Choose a Task: Select a task that you want to work on. Set a Timer: Set a timer for 25 minutes, called a "Pomodoro." Work on the Task: Focus on the chosen task without any distractions until the timer rings. Take a Short Break: Once the Pomodoro timer rings, take a short break, typically 5 minutes. Repeat: Repeat the process. After completing four Pomodoros, take a longer break, usually around 15-30 minutes.
